\begin{abstract}
We study the application of Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) and Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) to pixel-based continuous control tasks. While theoretically superior for continuous action spaces, PPO and SAC exhibit extreme hyperparameter instability when learning directly from high-dimensional visual observations. To address this, we leverage the Optuna framework to execute automated, short-horizon hyperparameter searches, rapidly identifying and pruning diverging configurations. We successfully scale these algorithms to full training horizons and compare their performance, sample efficiency, and stability against our prior Q-learning baselines.
\end{abstract}
